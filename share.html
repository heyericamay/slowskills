Share your Skills

Are you over 55 with sewing experience and want to share your craft?

Whether you sew by hand or machine, quilt, mend, or create from scratch, we are inviting seniors to pass on their knowledge to a new generation eager to learn the art of practical self-sufficiency.

To foster intergenerational wisdom sharing and relations, we are prioritizing seniors who have these skills and would be interested in sharing their knowledge in a small group setting.

Funding

Slow Skills Sessions 2025 is funded by the City of Toronto's Live Green Program as a Neighbourhood Climate Action Grant recipient. We have grant funding until November 2025, but we are interested in continuing to offer textile skills workshops and events beyond our grant (and very open to collaboration in this after 2025!)
  • $100 honorarium in cash to workshop facilitators
  • Up to $20 transportation reimbursement
  • $25 honorarium in cash to workshop volunteers (2 per workshop)
  • Barrier-reduction budget as necessary for PPE, translation services, etc
  • Also have budget for supplies, equipment, event space, and refreshments


About the Workshops (for facilitators)

Workshops are free for youth (15-29) in Toronto, and can be in-person or virtual (depending on the content). Seniors/workshop facilitators, we will work around your availability. Materials and supplies will be sourced by us.
  • Virtual: can be at your home, volunteers will come to set up the meeting technology and support with any questions or comments from participants who tune in virtually. Will ask to record the session as a resource for anyone who missed it.
  • In-Person: we'll book an accessible space for the workshop and have volunteers to assist with set-up and facilitation. Workshops would be small groups of no more than 10 participants.
Slow Skills Sessions is a project of the Eco-Just Youth Co-op and is supported by the City of Toronto
through Live Green Toronto and the Neighbourhood Climate Action Grants program.
